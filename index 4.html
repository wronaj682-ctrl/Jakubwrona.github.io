The Myth of the Hunter

In Greek mythology, Orion was a giant huntsman whom Zeus placed among the stars.

One story says he was the son of Poseidon and could walk on water. He boasted he could kill any beast on Earth. In response, Gaia (Mother Earth) sent a giant Scorpion (Scorpius) to defeat him.

"This is why Orion and Scorpius are never in the sky at the same time. As one rises in the East, the other sets in the West, fleeing across the heavens."

Part 3: Explanation & Culture

Ancient Navigation

Because Orion is located on the Celestial Equator, it was a crucial guide for sailors worldwide. It rises in the East and sets in the West, helping navigators orient themselves on the open ocean.

Cultural Stories

Egyptians: Saw Orion as Osiris, the god of rebirth.

Navajo: Called it "Atse Ats'oosi" (The First Slender One), representing protection.

Earth's Movement

We see Orion in winter because Earth is on the side of the Sun facing outward toward the spiral arm of the galaxy where Orion resides. In summer, the Sun blocks our view of it.
